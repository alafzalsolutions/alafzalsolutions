Best Notebooks for Students in 2025: Tested & Ranked

We put 12 popular notebooks through three weeks of real daily use — lectures, revision sessions, and everything in between. Here's our honest ranking of what's worth buying and what to skip.

Finding the right notebook sounds simple — until you're staring at an aisle of identical-looking options with no idea which one will survive a full semester. We did the hard work for you.

Over three weeks, we tested 12 notebooks across different paper types, binding styles, and price points. We wrote in them daily, tested them with multiple pen types, and noted how they held up over time.

We focused on three categories that matter most to students: paper quality, durability, and value for money. Here's the full list of notebooks we put through our testing process.

  • Moleskine Classic (A5, plain)
  • Muji Notebook (A5, ruled)
  • Clairefontaine Age Bag (A5, lined)
  • Appointed Co. Notebook (B5, dotted)

Runner-Up: Leuchtturm1917

If the Rhodia is slightly out of budget, the Leuchtturm1917 is the next best thing. It's lighter, comes in a wide range of colors, and includes numbered pages with an index — genuinely useful for revision notebooks.

A Note on the Paper

The paper (80gsm) is thinner than the Rhodia, and fountain pen users will notice slight ghosting with wet nibs. Ballpoint and fineliner users won't have any issues.

⚠️
Fountain Pen Users
If you write with a fountain pen, stick with the Rhodia. The Leuchtturm's 80gsm paper shows ghosting with wetter nibs.

Budget Pick: Muji Notebook

For students who need to buy several notebooks at once, the Muji Notebook is unbeatable value. The paper is smooth, the cover is minimal and clean, and the price is a fraction of the premium options.

  1. Buy in bulk — Muji often runs multi-pack discounts
  2. Stick to ballpoint or gel pens for best results
  3. Use one notebook per subject to stay organized

Final Verdict

There's no single perfect notebook for everyone — it depends on your pen, your budget, and how you use it. But based on our testing, here's the simple breakdown:

  • Best overall: Rhodia Webnotebook — worth every penny if paper quality matters
  • Best runner-up: Leuchtturm1917 — great features, wide color range
  • Best budget: Muji Notebook — unbeatable value for everyday use
