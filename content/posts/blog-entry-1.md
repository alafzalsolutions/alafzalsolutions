There is something deeply satisfying about finding the perfect pen — the kind that glides across paper without skipping, bleeds through nothing, and makes your handwriting look better than it actually is. After years of trial and error, I have narrowed down my absolute favorites.

## The Notebook: Leuchtturm1917 A5 Dotted

If you are just starting out with bullet journaling, the Leuchtturm1917 is where I would point you every single time. The dotted grid is subtle enough that it does not overpower your layouts, the paper is thick enough to handle most fountain pens without ghosting, and the numbered pages plus index at the front make organization a genuine pleasure rather than a chore.

The cover is sturdy without being rigid, and the two ribbon bookmarks have saved me countless times from losing my place mid-month.

## The Everyday Pen: Staedtler Triplus Fineliner

For everyday writing, headers, and detail work, the Staedtler Triplus Fineliner in 0.3mm is unbeatable. The triangular barrel prevents hand fatigue during long study sessions, the ink is vivid and consistent, and the cap-off time before the nib dries out is impressive — great for those of us who get distracted mid-sentence.

I keep a set of twelve colors on my desk at all times. The forest green and terracotta shades are my personal favorites.

## Highlighters: Zebra Mildliners

Before Mildliners, I thought I did not like highlighters. Then I realized I just did not like the harsh fluorescent ones that made my notes look like a construction site. Mildliners changed everything. The dual-tip design gives you a broad stroke for underlining and a fine tip for annotation, and the muted pastel palette complements rather than fights your ink colors.

The warm grays and sage greens work beautifully for shadow effects in hand lettering.

## Washi Tape: MT Masking Tape

No stationery roundup is complete without washi tape, and MT remains the gold standard. The paper is thin enough that it does not add bulk to your spreads, the adhesive repositions cleanly, and the print quality on their seasonal collections is consistently beautiful.

I use it for page borders, dividers, and occasionally to rescue a layout I am not happy with — covering a mess with a beautiful strip of washi is one of the small joys of this hobby.
