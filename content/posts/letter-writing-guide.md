My grandmother kept every letter she ever received, bound with ribbon in a shoebox at the top of her wardrobe. When she passed, my mother brought the box home and we spent an afternoon reading through decades of correspondence — news from relatives, notes from old friends, small domestic updates that had traveled across cities and countries.

None of it was remarkable in content. All of it was irreplaceable.

## Why Letters Matter Differently

There is something a handwritten letter communicates that no digital message can replicate: the evidence of time. The recipient knows you sat down, chose your paper, found your pen, and thought long enough to fill a page. That investment is legible in the object itself, before a single word is read.

Letters are also, by their nature, a different mode of thinking. When you cannot edit endlessly or send a quick follow-up in thirty seconds, you tend to write more carefully, more completely, and more honestly.

## Getting Started

You do not need expensive equipment to begin. A clean sheet of unlined A5 paper and a pen you write comfortably with is genuinely enough. But if you want to make the ritual feel special:

**Paper**: Clairefontaine makes lovely writing paper with just the right weight and surface tooth. The Triomphe range is a classic for good reason.

**Envelopes**: Matching envelopes are satisfying, but mismatched ones with a wax seal can be equally charming. I keep a small collection of kraft envelopes, cream envelopes, and occasionally something more colorful for celebratory letters.

**Pens**: For letters, I reach for a fountain pen almost every time. The ink flows in a way that makes cursive feel natural rather than labored. If fountain pens feel like a commitment, a good rollerball — the Uni-ball Signo in 0.7mm — is an excellent alternative.

## What to Write About

The anxiety about what to say stops many people before they begin. The answer is simpler than it seems: write what you would say if you were catching up over tea. What have you been reading? What has the weather been like? What small thing made you laugh this week?

The mundane, written down and sent across a distance, becomes unexpectedly tender.

## The Ritual of Sending

Part of what makes letter writing meaningful is that it asks something of you beyond typing. Writing the address by hand. Choosing a stamp. Sealing the envelope and taking it to the post box. Each small step is a little ceremony, a deliberate act of attention toward another person.

Start with one letter. Pick someone you have been meaning to reach out to and write to them instead of texting. See how it feels.
